# Equities versus CDI: Descriptive Evidence across Multiple End Dates

## Abstract
This study presents, in a strictly descriptive manner, the historical performance of three hypothetical buy-and-hold portfolios of Brazilian equities, labeled A, B, and C, without rebalancing, relative to the CDI (the primary benchmark) and to additional benchmarks (Ibovespa TR and the S&P 500 in BRL, in both PR and TR variants). Under pre-specified rules, the comparisons are conducted over fixed 5-, 10-, 15-, 20-, and 25-year windows, evaluated at three end dates (snapshots). In this context, Table 1 summarizes cumulative total return and CAGR; Table 2 summarizes risk metrics (annualized volatility, maximum drawdown, and risk-adjusted measures of excess return relative to the CDI), as well as monthly statistics; and Table 3 documents the temporal robustness of the CAGR spread across end dates. Overall, the equities-versus-CDI differential is shown to be sensitive to both the investment horizon and the end date, particularly over shorter windows.

**Keywords:** equities; CDI; fixed windows; buy-and-hold; risk and return.  
**JEL Classification:** G11; G12.
